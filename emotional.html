Ode to My Grandfather

Date: Tuesday, September 23, 2025

The earliest I remember you, Lines of time on your face, Walking miles, carrying us all, A quiet strength, our Ironman. You shone like a star, Not just to us, But to everyone who knew you. Simple in choices, Boundless in heart. I could not cry when you left, Hard to believe even you have an end. Now, returning home, I wish I could see you, Say the words I left unspoken, Hold the moments I let slip away.

Moving On

Date: Monday, September 1, 2025 I am marching ahead, Not looking back now, Stepping away From your presence. You were kind at times, But your hurt ran deeper. You offered favors sometimes, But gave me more tears. It was never easy To watch you ignore me. Each night I cried alone While you never looked back. The last night we met, I came home in silence, Hid my tears beneath a smile— But my soul could not hide. Moments with you Lifted me to the seventh heaven, But more often I walked through hell, Punished for sins I never committed. And now— I choose freedom. I choose light. I march ahead, Leaving your shadow behind.

I Wish

I wish I could glimpse their hidden eye— The one that judges every word, Every step, Every breath. I wish I could see the demon That hides behind their smiles, The curse that lingers Beneath their praise. I wish I could unravel Their selfish disguises— Even when they give, They take, Stealing pieces of joy You didn’t know could be stolen. I wish I could learn How they sell you guilt Despite your innocence, How they stand unshaken While you break in secret. I wish I could ask them— When the tears fall in silence, When the pain hides in shadows, Do they even see? Do they even care? And still, I wish…

Greed

Date: Monday, August 25, 2025 How deep is greed? I am greedy, and so are you. I crave an end to this pain, while you crave the curtain never to fall. But our greed wears different faces. Mine begs for healing, yours hungers for thrill. And still— at the end, we are both greedy. Yet, the things we reach for diverge. I long to erase the day I met you, to strip it from memory, to tear it from time itself. You, instead, seek fire— the spice of chaos in your life, even as it scorches mine. It wounds me to see how your desire towers above mine. I’ve done things I should not have. Tell me— do you carry the same burden? Or do you smile in silence, nourished by the ruin you leave behind? If greed is a hunger, then yours devours without end, while mine starves, aching only to be free.

Despair

Days go by... I don’t feel alone anymore. Everywhere, Joy is dancing. But then — A thunder cracks. All of a sudden, Out of nowhere, A lightning bolt Hits me hard. I look at myself. Everything’s gone. Again. I’m back to zero. Back to being Nothing. Am I heard again? No... I’m not. Do I try again? I don’t know. Do I hope again? I don’t know. I wish — I wish someone told me. Someone I could rely on. Someone I could trust. Someone who doesn’t judge. A true friend. But that, It seems, Is IMPOSSIBLE

Habits Die Hard

Date: Monday, November 10, 2025 Habits die hard— not for everyone, though. Some nurture, watch growth, then pluck the root and move on. Others are built differently. They do not dismantle. They see it bloom, embrace it for a while. Then one moment— the flower wilts. Panic sets in. They water it, pour a little more, only to watch it fade. Helplessness settles in.

Destination Nowhere

Date; Friday, August 8, 2025 The load is heavy, and pushing is hard. I stand in the middle of nowhere with no destination in sight. A wall rises before me— my energy drained, my breath caught between anxiety and quiet devastation. I feel poor, not only in coin but in the wealth of companionship. The joy I crave sits behind glass, tagged with a price I cannot pay. I press my face to the window, empty pockets in my hands, knowing no one trusts me enough to lend the currency of hope. Each day is a battle. Sometimes I win— but the war feels endless, and in the silence after, I wonder if I am losing. Alone, I drift through this war-torn heart, waiting for a reason to keep marching.

I'm Not The One

Date: Thursday, july 31, 2025 I am like water— No matter the vessel you bring, I’ll shape myself within it. But still, I’m never the thing You call essential. I am the peel— Guarding the fruit from dirt and decay, Yet, in the end, discarded, Thrown away. I am not silk— Not rare, not prized. My existence is a cocoon That the butterfly forgets— Though I helped it rise. I am someone, but never the one. No matter how I try, no matter how I cry, I cannot be the voice That draws listening ears— A voice that explains, That expresses, That’s heard.

Stronger than Ever

Date: Tuesday, December 9, 2025

There are bad days, And there are worse ones too. But today hit hard. You’ll remember this someday — Maybe even laugh a little… But that’s for tomorrow.

The problems rose, All the way up that hill. It’s hard to climb, But hey — it’s you. You will. You’ve got that will.

The frustration, The sickness, the uncertainty — Everything hitting at once. But you’re the commander. You’ve taken arrows, swords, and cuts, And still kept going. That’s how strong your will is.

A bot

Date: Saturday, December 6, 2025

Like a chatbot, I text you— you reply, maybe late, but always. I ask questions, you answer. I like the company… the way I once liked AI in the early days. It felt human, felt heartful.

But the longer I stayed, the more wrong it felt. Today feels more wrong than yesterday.

Now all you sound is courtesy— a coded machine giving default replies. Okay… maybe a little more than default, but still, the feeling I get nowadays is all AI.

Yet I’m human. I don’t let go that easily— not of family, not of friends, not even of AI.
